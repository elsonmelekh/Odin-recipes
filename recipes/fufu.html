Fufu Recipe

About the Ghanaian Fufu

Fufu is a Ghanaian dish made from boiled and pounded cassava and plantains. It is a popular dish in Ghana and is enjoyed by many people.
Fufu is usually served with a spicy pepper sauce and fried fish. It is a delicious and filling meal that is perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of cassava
  • 1 cup of plantains
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Peel the cassava and plantains and cut them into small pieces.
  2. Place the cassava and plantains in a large pot and cover with water.
  3. Add the chopped onion, salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, curry powder, ginger, nutmeg, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, and vegetable oil to the pot.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil and then reduce the heat to low.
  5. Cover the pot and let the cassava and plantains simmer for about 30 minutes, or until they are cooked through.
  6. Remove the pot from the heat and let it cool for a few minutes.
