Wakye Recipe

About the Ghanaian Wakye

Wakye is a Ghanaian dish of cooked rice and beans. It is a popular dish in Ghana and is enjoyed by many people.
Wakye is usually served with a spicy pepper sauce and fried plantains. It is a delicious and filling meal that is perfect for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of rice
  • 1 cup of black-eyed peas
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of paprika
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon of onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger
  • 1 teaspoon of nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon of cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon of vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. Wash the rice and beans in cold water.
  2. Place the rice and beans in a large pot and cover with water.
  3. Add the chopped onion, salt, pepper, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, thyme, curry powder, ginger, nutmeg, cinnamon, cayenne pepper, and vegetable oil to the pot.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil and then reduce the heat to low.
  5. Cover the pot and let the rice and beans simmer for about 30 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the beans are tender.
  6. Stir the rice and beans occasionally to prevent them from sticking to the bottom of the pot.
  7. Once the rice and beans are cooked, remove the pot from the heat and let it sit for a few minutes before serving.
  8. Serve the wakye with a spicy pepper sauce and fried plantains.
